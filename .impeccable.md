# Leo's Notes

## Design Context

### Users
Readers who want to discover Leo's writing, notes, and technical or creative work. The primary task is comfortable long-form reading and simple exploration by topic.

### Brand Personality
Personal, precise, and playful. The site should feel authored rather than productized, with the warmth of a well-kept notebook and the clarity of a technical reference.

### Aesthetic Direction
An original pixel-inspired editorial site informed by the hard edges, compact navigation, and content-first structure of taniarascia.com. Use a warm off-white light theme, ink-dark typography, and brick-red interaction accents, with an equivalent dark theme. Avoid gradients, frosted glass, soft shadows, oversized hero copy, and generic dashboard cards.

### Design Principles
- Put the reading experience and useful navigation ahead of decoration.
- Treat the pixel language as a precise system: integer spacing, hard borders, and deliberate hover states.
- Make every page usable on mobile without hiding core content or navigation.
- Keep interactive motion short, purposeful, and compatible with reduced-motion preferences.
- Build reusable visual primitives so future notes, projects, and experiments remain recognizably part of the same site.
